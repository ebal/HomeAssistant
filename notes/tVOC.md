What is the **VOC Index** on VINDSTYRKA?

**Purpose**: The sensor uses an algorithm to track rapid changes in VOCs, often compared to how a human nose adapts to odors over time.

**Scale**: 1–500 (unitless index)  
**Baseline**: 100 = average VOC level over the past 24 hours

| Index Range | Interpretation |
|-------------|---------------|
| **1–99** | Cleaner air than recent average (e.g., after ventilation) |
| **100** | Baseline: average VOC background over last 24h |
| **101–199** | Slight increase in VOC activity |
| **200–249** | Moderate increase |
| **250–349** | Significant increase |
| **350–500** | Severe increase / strong VOC event |


**Meaning**

- ~ 100: Normal, clean air.
- < 100: Improved air quality.
- \> 100: Increasing VOC presence (needs ventilation or air purification). 

**tVOC Concentration**

The arrows represent the VOC concentrations

* tVOC ⬆️ : increasing 
* tVOC ➡️ : stable 
* tVOC ⬇️ : descreasing
